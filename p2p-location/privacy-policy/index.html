Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 8, 2026

P2P Location is a two-person location sharing app. This Privacy Policy explains what information the app processes, how it is used, and what choices you have.

Information the app processes

  • Location data that you choose to share during an active session.
  • Session data such as session IDs, session tokens, session expiration time, and connection state.
  • Basic app content you enter, such as session names or display names used for invitations.
  • Diagnostic and crash information through Firebase Crashlytics.
  • App analytics and remote configuration data through Firebase services.

How location sharing works

The app is designed for private, person-to-person location sharing between two devices. When a session is active, your device may send location updates directly to the other participant using a peer-to-peer WebRTC connection. The app's backend server is used mainly for signaling, which helps the two devices discover each other and establish the connection.

Session links and codes are used so another person can join your session. Anyone with a valid invite link or code may be able to request access to the session until it expires or is deleted.

The app is intended so that your live location remains private to the participant you choose to share with. The signaling server is not designed to carry or store live location payloads during normal operation.

What is stored on your device

The app stores session information locally on your device so sessions can be restored, displayed in the app, and managed after creation or joining. This local information may include:

  • session IDs and session tokens,
  • session names,
  • recent shared coordinates and timestamps,
  • session status and expiration details.

What is processed by backend services

The app uses backend services to create and join sessions and to coordinate peer-to-peer WebRTC connections. These services may process session identifiers, temporary session tokens, and limited connection metadata needed to establish a session.

Sessions are intended to expire automatically after their configured duration unless deleted earlier. The backend is used mainly for session creation, joining, and signaling rather than as the primary transport for live location updates.

Firebase services

The app uses Firebase services, including:

  • Firebase Crashlytics for crash reporting and diagnostics,
  • Firebase Analytics for usage analytics,
  • Firebase Remote Config for configuration values used by the app.

These services may collect device, app, and diagnostic information in accordance with Google's policies.

Permissions

The app may request permissions such as:

  • precise and approximate location,
  • background location,
  • notifications,
  • internet access.

These permissions are used to support location sharing, session management, and app notifications.

How your information is used

  • To create, join, and manage location sharing sessions.
  • To transmit location updates between session participants.
  • To show session state, recent activity, and connection status in the app.
  • To troubleshoot crashes, improve reliability, and operate the service.

Data sharing

Your shared location is intended to be visible to the other participant in the session. The app also uses service providers such as Firebase and backend infrastructure needed to operate the app.

Data retention

Session data may remain on your device until you delete it or clear app data. Backend session data is intended to be temporary and may be removed when a session expires or is deleted.

Your choices

  • You can choose whether to grant location and notification permissions.
  • You can stop using the app, delete sessions, or uninstall the app.
  • You can manage some permissions at any time in your device settings.

Children's privacy

P2P Location is not intended for children under 13, and the app is not knowingly designed to collect personal information from children under 13.

Changes to this policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated from time to time. The updated version will be posted on this page with a new effective date.
